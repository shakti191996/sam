Sam Manekshaw

First field marshal of India

Field Marshal Sam Hormusji Framji Jamshedji Manekshaw, (3 April 1914 – 27 June 2008)

Here's a time line of Sam Manekshaw:

  • 1914 - Born in punjab , India.
  • 1932 - Joined British Indian Army
  • 1934 - Promoted to Second Lieutenant in British Indian Army.
  • 1936 - Promoted to Lieutenant in British Indian Army.
  • 1940 - Promoted to Captian in British Indian Army.
  • 1940 - Promoted to Major in British Indian Army.
  • 1944 - Promoted to Lieutenant colonel in British Indian Army.
  • 1947 - Promoted to Major in Indian Army.
  • 1948 - Promoted to Colonel in Indian Army.
  • 1948 - Promoted to Brigadier in Indian Army.
  • 1957 - Promoted to Major General in Indian Army.
  • 1962 - Promoted to Lieutenant General in Indian Army.
  • 1969 - Promoted to General in Indian Army.
  • 1972 - honored with the Padma Vibhushan, the second highest civilian award in the Republic of India
  • 1973 - Promoted to Field Marshal in Indian Army.
  • 2008 - Dies at the age of 94

"A ‘Yes man’ is a dangerous man. He is a menace. He will go very far. He can become a minister, a secretary or a Field Marshall but he can never become a leader nor, ever be respected. He will be used by his superiors, disliked by his colleagues and despised by his subordinates. So discard the ‘Yes man’."

-- Sam Manekshaw
